Course Selection

For the architects of learning course, I was asked to choose a course that I regularly teach to apply the class concepts to. Although I am not the course lead, I chose CSE 170 - Technical Teamwork. I previously made updates to this class with the permission of the course lead and wanted to continue the work I did previously.

Course Vision Statement

"Technical Teamwork teaches you emotional intelligence and teamwork skills you need to function well at work with others who might not be like you."

Course Revision Initial Goals

My goal is to more closely align activities and assignments to outcomes with the framework of emotional intelligence as the backdrop. The course council felt that some assignments could be better formulated. This also required better weekly outcomes which align to course outcomes.

Specifically, each assignment can be traced back to one or more elements of emotional intelligence. Each assignment has a meaningful rubric which helps us know if the student met the weekly outcome.

I will collaborate with the course council for CSE 170, as well as my teammates and instructors in the AoL class.

Course Revision Plan

Timeline with major steps included:

  • Week 5-6: Identify Weekly Objectives that align with course objectives. Ensure weekly objectives include the language of emotional intelligence.
  • Week 7-8: Audit weekly assignments and activities to determine if assignments effectively meet weekly objectives. Identify alternative activities for weeks that are not in alignment.
  • Week 9-10: Evaluate assignment rubrics to determine if adjustments are needed to ensure alignment with emotional intelligence goals and outcomes.
  • Week 11-12: Holistic review of class adjustments and final review by course council.

Resources needed

Time to do this review.

People who will be assisting you

CSE 170 Course Council: Matt Manley, Kristi Hansen (lead), Christopher Keers
